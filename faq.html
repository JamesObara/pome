Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about touring Kenya with Peterson. Can't find your answer? Feel free to contact us!

Yes! Airport pickup and drop-off is included in all our tour packages. Peterson will meet you at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) with a personalized sign. We also provide pickup from Wilson Airport for domestic flights. Please provide your flight details at least 24 hours in advance.

All our tours are private and personalized for you and your group. This means you'll have Peterson as your dedicated guide, and the itinerary can be customized to your interests and pace. We don't combine different groups, ensuring a more intimate and flexible experience.

We accept multiple payment methods for your convenience: cash (USD or Kenyan Shillings), mobile money (M-Pesa), bank transfers, and major credit cards. A 30% deposit is required to confirm your booking, with the balance due on the day of the tour. We'll provide secure payment instructions upon booking confirmation.

For safari tours, we recommend: comfortable clothing in neutral colors (khaki, brown, green), a hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, insect repellent, camera with extra batteries, binoculars (we can provide if needed), and a light jacket for early morning drives. We provide bottled water and can arrange packed lunches.

Yes, Nairobi is safe when you're with an experienced local guide like Peterson. We know which areas to visit and avoid, and we take all necessary precautions for your safety. Our vehicles are well-maintained and insured, and Peterson is trained in first aid. We also stay updated on current conditions and adjust routes as needed.

Kenya is a year-round destination! The dry seasons (January-March and June-October) are ideal for wildlife viewing as animals gather around water sources. The Great Migration in Maasai Mara typically occurs July-October. However, each season offers unique experiences - the green season (April-May and November-December) has fewer crowds and beautiful landscapes.

Peterson specializes in creating custom experiences based on your interests. Whether you're passionate about photography, birdwatching, cultural experiences, conservation, or adventure activities, we can design a tour that matches your preferences. Just let us know what excites you most about Kenya.

Yes, meals are included in full-day tours and multi-day packages. We can accommodate dietary restrictions and preferences - just let us know in advance. For day tours, we typically include lunch at carefully selected local restaurants or scenic picnic spots. We're happy to introduce you to authentic Kenyan cuisine!

Kenya's weather is generally favorable year-round, but if severe weather affects your planned activities, we'll work with you to modify the itinerary or reschedule. We have backup indoor activities and alternative routes prepared. Your safety and enjoyment are our top priorities, and we'll ensure you have a great experience regardless of weather conditions.

We recommend booking at least 2-3 weeks in advance, especially during peak seasons (July-October and December-January). However, we can often accommodate last-minute requests depending on availability. For special events like the Great Migration or holiday periods, earlier booking (1-2 months) is advisable to ensure the best experience.

Yes! We offer 2-7 day safari packages to destinations like Maasai Mara, Amboseli, Lake Nakuru, and Tsavo. These include accommodation (from budget camping to luxury lodges), all meals, park fees, and transportation. Multi-day safaris provide the best wildlife viewing opportunities and allow you to experience Kenya's diverse ecosystems.

Our tour prices include: professional guide services, transportation in a well-maintained safari vehicle, park entrance fees, bottled water, and meals as specified in each tour. Airport transfers are complimentary. Additional costs might include accommodation (for multi-day tours), alcoholic beverages, personal shopping, and tips (which are appreciated but not mandatory).
